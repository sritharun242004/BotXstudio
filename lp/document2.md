## The Core Idea

Every fashion brand needs great product photos. Getting them today means hiring a photographer, booking a model, renting a studio, and waiting days for edited results — all for images that may need reshoots anyway.

BotStudioX removes every one of those steps. Upload your garment, get a studio-quality photo in under a minute. That is the single idea the landing page must land.

## Design Notes

**Visual language:**
- Clean, fashion-forward aesthetic — white backgrounds, generous whitespace
- High-contrast before/after visuals wherever possible
- Real output images beat stock photos every time

**Color palette:**
- Primary: deep charcoal or navy (premium, trust)
- Accent: gold or warm amber (fashion industry association)
- Background: white and near-white sections, alternated for rhythm

**Typography:**
- Headlines: a serif or high-contrast sans-serif (Playfair Display, DM Serif, or similar)
- Body: clean sans-serif (Inter, DM Sans)

**Mobile first:**
- Hero CTA must be thumb-reachable on mobile
- The step-by-step workflow section collapses to a vertical scroll on small screens
- Output gallery uses a 2-column grid on mobile

---

## Copy Principles

1. **Lead with outcomes, not features.** Say "publish a new product today" not "use our AI image generation pipeline."
2. **Quantify wherever possible.** "Under 60 seconds" and "₹2,000 per garment savings" are more persuasive than "fast" and "affordable."
3. **Use the word 'you' often.** The page is about the buyer's business, not our technology.
4. **No tech jargon.** Words like "AI model," "pipeline," "composite," "Gemini," or "multi-stage" never appear on the landing page.
5. **Short sentences.** Fashion buyers scan, they don't read. If a sentence can be cut in half, cut it.
